Evaluation Protocol (Instructional/Technical)Title: Evaluation Procedure for Log-Transformed Time-Series ModelsTo ensure the accuracy of the predictive results and maintain the physical significance of the error metrics, a rigorous three-step evaluation protocol is applied to all models. Because the models are trained on a log-transformed target variable—$y_{log} = \log(1 + \text{Patients})$—to address the 41.91% zero-inflation and extreme right-skewness of the dengue data, the inference phase follows this specific sequence:Log-Space Prediction: The model generates an initial forecast in the logarithmic scale: $y_{pred, log} = \text{model.predict}(X_{test})$.Inverse Transformation: To revert to the original unit of "patient counts," the log-prediction is transformed using the exponential minus one function: $y_{pred, raw} = \exp(y_{pred, log}) - 1$. In Python, this is executed as np.expm1(y_pred_log).Physical Metric Calculation: The back-transformed predictions ($y_{pred, raw}$) are compared against the ground-truth raw patient counts ($y_{test, raw}$) to calculate real-world performance metrics: Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), and R-Squared ($R^2$).2. Methodology Summary (Phase 2: Modeling)Section 2: Predictive Modeling and OptimizationFor this research, six diverse machine learning algorithms were selected to provide a comprehensive comparison between linear baselines and non-linear ensemble power. These models are:LightGBM: The primary champion model, selected for its leaf-wise growth strategy which provides superior accuracy and computational efficiency for outbreak detection.CatBoost: Implemented for its advanced handling of categorical variables (such as administrative divisions) and its innate resistance to overfitting.XGBoost: A high-performance gradient boosting baseline used to benchmark the advancements of newer boosting techniques.Random Forest: A bagging-based ensemble baseline used to evaluate the robustness of traditional decision-tree architectures.Support Vector Regression (SVR): A kernel-based model utilized to evaluate non-linear mapping effectiveness using the Radial Basis Function (RBF).Ridge Regression: A regularized linear baseline used to establish the fundamental performance floor for the study.All models (excluding Ridge) underwent Hyperparameter Optimization (HPO) via Randomized Search with 5-Fold Time-Series Cross-Validation. The optimization objective was the minimization of Root Mean Squared Logarithmic Error (RMSLE), ensuring that the models are robust to the exponential growth patterns of peak dengue seasons.3. Evaluation Condition Table (Text Description)Table: Comparative Evaluation LogicAll six models in this study follow a unified evaluation logic to ensure consistency across comparisons:Prediction State: All models predict directly in the log-scale ($y_{pred, log}$).Inverse Transform Requirement: Yes, all predictions must be back-transformed via np.expm1() to reach the original patient count scale.Physical Metric Comparison: Metrics such as MAE, RMSE, and $R^2$ are calculated by comparing back-transformed predictions ($y_{pred, raw}$) against original patient counts ($y_{test, raw}$).Statistical Metric Comparison: The RMSLE is calculated by comparing $y_{pred, log}$ directly with the log-transformed ground truth ($y_{test, log}$), providing a robust assessment that is less sensitive to the high variance of epidemic peaks.4.
